Associate

An associate degree is an undergraduate academic degree that's earned after completing two to three years of post-secondary study: Education An associate degree is a two-year degree that focuses on foundational or technical education. It's an academic qualification that falls between a high school diploma and a bachelor's degree. Coursework To earn an associate degree, students must complete at least 60 semester-units of coursework, including general education courses, electives, and at least 18 units in a major area. Schools Associate degrees can be obtained from community colleges, junior colleges, online universities, or some four-year institutions. Career paths An associate degree can open up new job opportunities, or it can be used as a stepping stone to a bachelor's degree program. It can also be a good way to improve a resume or change careers for established professionals. Cost An associate's degree is usually less expensive than a bachelor's degree. At public colleges, an associate's degree costs about $10,000, while a bachelor's degree costs about $19,500.

Boeuf Bourguignon

Boeuf Bourguignon is a classic French dish known for its rich, hearty flavors. Traditionally made with tender beef slow-cooked in red wine, it's infused with aromatic herbs, garlic, and vegetables like carrots and onions. This comforting stew hails from the Burgundy region, where wine and local ingredients shine in every bite. It’s a timeless recipe that embodies the essence of French country cooking, perfect for a cozy, flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • Red wine: 2 cups (500 ml) of red wine (preferably Burgundy wine)

  • Beef: 1 kg (about 2.2 lb) of braising beef (such as chuck or brisket)

  • Beef broth: 1 cup (250 ml) of beef broth

  • Flour: 2 tablespoons of flour

  • Olive oil: 2 to 3 tablespoons of olive oil

  • Butter: 1 tablespoon of butter

  • Onions: 2 medium onions

  • Garlic: 3 cloves of garlic

  • Bay leaf: 1 bay leaf

  • Thyme: 2 sprigs of fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon of dried thyme)

  • Carrots: 2 to 3 medium carrots (about 200 g or 7 oz)

  • Mushrooms: 8.8 oz (250 g) of button mushrooms

  • Salt and pepper: to taste

Directions

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Cut 1 kg (2.2 lb) of beef into large chunks, slice the 2 to 3 carrots, chop the 2 onions, mince the 3 cloves of garlic, and clean the 250 g (8.8 oz) of mushrooms.

  2. Brown the Beef: Heat 2 to 3 tablespoons of olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven. Add the beef chunks and brown them on all sides. Once browned, remove the beef and set it aside.

  3. Sauté Vegetables: In the same pot, add the chopped onions and carrots. Cook them until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute.

  4. Add Flour: Sprinkle 2 tablespoons of flour over the vegetables and stir well to coat everything. This will help thicken the stew.

  5. Add Wine & Broth: Pour in 500 ml (2 cups) of red wine and 250 ml (1 cup) of beef broth. Stir to combine. Return the browned beef to the pot.

  6. Season & Simmer: Add 2 sprigs of thyme, 1 bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for 2.5 to 3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the beef is tender.

  7. Sauté Mushrooms: About 30 minutes before the beef is done, heat 1 tablespoon of butter in a pan and sauté the mushrooms until golden brown. Add them to the stew.

  8. Serve: Once the beef is tender and the flavors have melded, remove the thyme and bay leaf. Serve your Boeuf Bourguignon hot, accompanied by potatoes, pasta, or crusty bread.
